    • MS Causes, Symptoms and Consequences

    Based on my studies, experience in working with numerous MS patients and especially living and treating my wife for more than 17 years, I have gathered a unique and intimate knowledge and understanding of the causes and triggers for various MS symptoms as well as consequences it brings to patients, their families and caretakers. Living and breathing the distress and hardships patients go through and working with them for almost two decades now I have been able to set up and refine a unique and successful therapy giving consistent and impressive results. Here, I will atempt to summarize the most important insights that will explain why and how the therapy system was established.
    One of the common precursors among MS patients lies in wrong attitude and behavior towards oneself. Most often, individuals are not aware of this. Treating themselves that way is destructive to their body. Their body is very durable, especially when it’s young and able to withstand the abuse they inflict to it, even for decades, but one day it’s just no longer able to withstand this, and this is manifested in symptoms at the places where they are most sensitive. In MS patients, these places are nerves. It could be that they have been treating themselves that way their entire lives. It does not matter if such behavior is a consequence of education, material status, environment, social imposition, or simply a consequence of personality.
    Therefore, the causes lie is psychology, that is, in the lack of observation and fulfillment of their needs, desires, interests, etc. Hence, this is not a disease, but a condition, so I will refer to it as such bellow.
    Usually, the first significant symptoms are caused by a stressful situation. This may not necessarily be a terrible event happening, but rather an unfortunate happenstance that has led to the appearance of symptoms under the influence of their bad moods, negative things happening to them in succession, or even something else. So, the stress itself is not the trigger so much as their reaction to the stress. Symptoms can be different and almost always vary from person to person. Therefore, neurologists often diagnose various atypical forms of MS (in addition to the 4 basic types according to the way the disease acts) but it is, in fact, all the same condition. Symptoms that accompany this condition are drastic, because they affect our everyday life, and therefore they lead to fear.
    Fear is a fundamental consequence accompanying the development of a progressive condition, especially when going to a neurologist with your symptoms and having MS diagnosed as an incurable disease, for which neither the cause, the course, nor the cure are known. This fear becomes an integral part of life of patients with this condition. Because it feels like such a hopeless situation and because the symptoms make you feel insecure, weak, and helpless, diagnosis often leads to depression. It can cause you to close yourself within your house because no one can help you, nor can they understand your pain. It may even become so that you hide away from people’s eyes because they bother you. The fear leads to unimaginable psycho-somatic consequences, which lead to various problems, and even physical pain.
