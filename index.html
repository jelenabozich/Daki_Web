    MS Causes, Symptoms and Consequences

    Based on my studies, experience in working with numerous MS patients and especially living and treating my wife for more than 17 years, I have gathered a unique and intimate knowledge and understanding of the causes and triggers for various MS symptoms as well as consequences it brings to patients, their families and caretakers. Living and breathing the distress and hardships patients go through and working with them for almost two decades now I have been able to set up and refine a unique and successful therapy giving consistent and impressive results. Here, I will atempt to summarize the most important insights that will explain why and how the therapy system was established.
    One of the common precursors among MS patients lies in wrong attitude and behavior towards oneself. Most often, individuals are not aware of this. Treating ourselves that way is destructive to our body. Our body is very durable, especially when it’s young and able to withstand the abuse we inflict it, even for decades, but one day it’s just no longer able to do it, and this is manifested in symptoms at the places where we are most sensitive. In MS patients, these places are nerves. It could be that we’ve been treating ourselves that way our entire lives. It does not matter if such behavior is a consequence of education, material status, environment, social imposition, or simply a consequence of personality.
    Therefore, the causes lie is psychology, that is, in the lack of observation and fulfillment of our needs, desires, interests, etc. Hence, this is not a disease, but a condition, so I will refer to it as such bellow.
    Usually, the first significant symptomsstrong> are caused by a stressful situation. This may not necessarily be a terrible event happening, but rather an unfortunate happenstance that has led to the appearance of symptoms under the influence of our bad moods, negative things happening to us in succession, or even something else. So, the stress itself is not the trigger so much as our reaction to the stress. Symptoms can be different and almost always vary from person to person. Therefore, neurologists often diagnose various atypical forms of MS (in addition to the 4 basic types according to the way the disease acts) but it is, in fact, all the same condition. Symptoms that accompany this condition are drastic, because they affect our everyday life, and therefore they lead to fear.
    Fearstrong> is a fundamental consequencestrong> accompanying the development of a progressive condition, especially when going to a neurologist with your symptoms and having MS diagnosed as an incurable disease, for which neither the cause, the course, nor the cure are known. This fear becomes an integral part of life of patients with this condition. Because it feels like such a hopeless situation and because the symptoms make you feel insecure, weak, and helpless, diagnosis often leads to depression. It can cause you to close yourself within your house because no one can help you, nor can they understand your pain. It may even become so that you hide away from people’s eyes because they bother you. The fear leads to unimaginable psycho-somatic consequences, which lead to various problems, and even physical pain.

    Therapy

    In order to determine the necessary therapy and its approximate length, it is necessary to agree on the shortest time that we can see through Skype to establish an anamnesis. The patient can then decide whether and when to apply the therapy that is being used here. It is noted that the camera that displays the patient should be distant from the patient as much as it takes to see it in its entirety. (in a sitting or lying position) The therapy in the first week consists of four parts.
  • Talk with the patient about his symptoms, psychological profile, the environment in which he lives and general introduction.
  • Bio energetic therapy with which the aura (which represents an energy indicator) complements, balances and regenerates in the areas where the symptoms are expressed.
  • Psychotherapy, which changes the image of its capabilities, the experience of external situations and events, changing habits and perception, fulfilling real personal needs.
  • Physical therapy that achieves movements that are difficult or impossible for the patient because of the condition in which they are present. In the second week and further (if needed), the therapy is done without bioenergy therapy, that is, with psychotherapy and physical therapy.Therapy should not take place in this order or that all four parts of therapy, but the emphasis is put on the individual needs of each patient.In the first week, the therapy takes place for 5 days in a row (Monday to Friday) at the same time each day, which is agreed with the patient prior to the arrangement. The duration of the daily therapy is 1 hour and a maximum of 1 hour and 30 minutes.
  • It is especially emphasized that without the patient’s actual will to change behavior and habits this therapy will not have complete success. That’s why the therapy is exclusively done and scheduled with a patient, not with a cousin, a friend or any other person. They should be present if the patient needs help or curiosity for any reason. The therapy is conducted in Serbian and English, and may also be in French.
